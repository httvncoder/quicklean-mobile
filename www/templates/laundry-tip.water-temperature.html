When to use hot water

For whites, typical dirty clothes and diapers, use hot water (130° F). Hot water is best for removing germs and heavy soiling. However, hot water can shrink, fade, and damage some fabrics. So, be sure to read your clothing labels before selecting the hot option.

When to use warm water

For artificial fibers, knits, and jeans, use warm water (90° F). Most of your clothes can be washed in warm water. It offers good cleaning without significant fading or shrinking.

When to use cold water

For dark, bright colors that bleed or has delicate fabrics, use cold water (80° F). Cold water also saves energy, so it is a good choice if you want to be eco-friendly. If you want to use cold water, you may need to pre-treat or pre-soak your clothes if your laundry items are heavily stained.
